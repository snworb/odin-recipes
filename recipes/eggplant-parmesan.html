Eggplant Parmesan

Description

Eggplant parmesan is a classic Italian dish made with sliced eggplant that's breaded and fried until crispy, then layered with tomato sauce and melted mozzarella cheese. It's typically served as a main course and is often baked in the oven to create a gooey, cheesy, and delicious meal. The eggplant is typically sliced into rounds, dusted with flour, dipped in beaten eggs, and then coated with breadcrumbs before being fried. This creates a crispy exterior that contrasts perfectly with the tender eggplant interior.

Variations on the dish may include the use of different types of cheese, the addition of herbs like basil or oregano, or the use of different types of sauce like marinara or arrabiata. Regardless of the variations, eggplant parmesan is a popular dish that's loved for its flavor and texture, and it's a staple in many Italian-American homes.

Ingredients

  • 3 large eggplant, peeled and thinly sliced
  • 2 large eggs, beaten
  • 4 cups Italian seasoned bread crumbs
  • 6 cups spaghetti sauce, divided
  • 1 (16 ounce) package mozzarella cheese, shredded divided
  • 1/2 cup grated Parmesan cheese, divided
  • 1/2 teaspoon dried basil

Steps

  1. Preheat oven to 350 degrees F (175 degrees C).
  2. Dip eggplant sliced in beated egg, then in bread crumbs to coat. Place in a single layer on a baking sheet.
  3. Spread spaghetti sauce to cover the bottom of a 9x13-inch baking dish. Place a layer of eggplant slices in the sauce. Sprinkle with mozzarella and Parmesan cheese, ending with a cheese layer. Sprinkle basil on top.
  4. Bake in the preheated oven until golden brown, about 35 minutes.
